Pills

How to use it:

Birth control pills, also known as oral contraceptives, are taken daily at around the same time. There are different types of pills, including combination pills (containing estrogen and progestin) and progestin-only pills (mini-pills).

Side effects:

Common side effects can include nausea, breast tenderness, irregular bleeding, and mood changes. Some people may experience changes in their menstrual cycle, weight, or libido. Serious side effects are rare but can include blood clots, high blood pressure, and increased risk of certain cancers.

Treatment:

If you have concerns about the pill or experience side effects, it's important to discuss them with your healthcare provider. They can assess your situation and suggest alternative pill formulations or other contraceptive methods if needed.

More info:

Birth control pills are highly effective when taken correctly. They provide additional benefits such as regularizing menstrual cycles and reducing the risk of certain conditions like ovarian cysts and endometrial cancer. However, they do not protect against STIs. It's important to consult with a healthcare professional to discuss the suitability of different contraceptive methods based on your individual health and circumstances.
